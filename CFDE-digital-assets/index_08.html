Interactive Sankey: CFDE Program Digital Assets
& Their Potential Host Repositories

Background

Under the leadership and guidance of NIH POs, and in close collaboration with CFDE participating programs, the CFDE Integration and Coordination Center – Sustainability Core (ICC-SC) has developed projects to achieve three major milestones: (1) Assess CF program needs; (2) collaborate with their Data Resource Centers (DRCs) to render CF digital assets FAIR; and (3) promote outreach and reuse of CF digital assets, amplify CFDE impact, and support CFDE sustainability.

Our project presented here is a major effort to support all three milestones by conducting preliminary analyses of existing digital assets generated from CFDE programs and providing suggestions for future data hosts of these assets, visualized in an interactive Sankey diagram. To support these efforts, the project will establish a comprehensive data repository infrastructure to accommodate diverse biomedical datasets from CFDE programs. By implementing standardized metadata schemas, interoperable APIs, and robust storage solutions, we create a unified ecosystem where heterogeneous data types—including genomic sequences, imaging datasets, clinical measurements, and multi-omics profiles—can be effectively maintained, integrated, and accessed. Our framework prioritizes the FAIR principles to ensure seamless data sharing and reuse, addressing the critical challenge of data fragmentation across research communities. This initiative will facilitate cross-program data analysis, uncovering novel insights that might otherwise remain hidden in isolated repositories. By reducing barriers to data discovery and reuse, this project will accelerate biomedical research, maximize the return on NIH’s substantial investments in data generation, and contribute to the sustainability and commercialization of CFDE resources

Approach

Our methodology focuses on efficient data categorization and repository allocation through metadata-driven analysis. We begin by extracting critical data type information directly from the most recent C2M2 metadata files accompanying each dataset from NIH CFDE programs. This automated extraction process identifies key attributes including data modality, format, structure, and associated experimental protocols.

Once extracted, we employ a hierarchical clustering algorithm to group similar data types based on their technical and biological characteristics. This grouping strategy enables us to identify natural collections of related data that should be stored together to maximize usability and cross-referencing capabilities.

Each identified data group is then systematically mapped to appropriate repository infrastructures based on optimal storage parameters, access requirements, and analytical tools needed for that specific data type. This mapping process considers factors such as storage capacity, query performance, security requirements, and compatibility with domain-specific analysis pipelines.

The entire data flow—from source datasets through extraction, grouping, and repository assignment—is visualized through an interactive Sankey diagram in our prototype. The diagram dynamically illustrates the proportion of data flowing into each repository type, highlighting distribution patterns and potential bottlenecks in the infrastructure. This visualization will help stakeholders understand how diverse biomedical data streams are organized and where specific data types are stored, providing an intuitive interface to facilitate the identification of opportunities for enhanced data management, integration, and sustainability.
